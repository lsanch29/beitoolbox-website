Energy Metrics

Energy metrics summarize how energy is allocated across brain regions and how regions covary in their energy fluctuations. In the BEI Toolbox, energy metrics are computed at the study level so that estimation and inference are performed on a fixed cohort definition and are directly comparable across diagnosis groups.

Scope

Design decision

The toolbox does not need to compute population energy metrics for standalone datasets. Dataset preprocessing produces PDO files; population energy modeling is performed on studies.

This separation enforces a clear scientific boundary: subject artifacts are deterministic transforms of raw recordings, while population artifacts depend on cohort definition, group labeling, and inferential choices.

Estimands

Energy metrics in this toolbox generally target two estimands:

Distribution of windowed energy

For each region, estimate the empirical distribution of windowed energy samples pooled across subjects in a group. This supports modeling heavy tails and testing equal energy allocation.

Cross-region covariance structure

Estimate covariance or correlation between regional energy time series, either within subject then pooled, or directly on pooled representations, depending on the metric definition.

Energy time series and subject metrics

The energy-metrics pipeline in the toolbox is two-stage:

  1. Compute a windowed energy time series for each subject and region by aggregating signal amplitude over consecutive analysis windows (for example using the segmentation returned by file_analysis). The resulting energy matrix is then normalized by its global maximum so values lie in [0,1].
  2. Compute per-subject metrics from the normalized energy matrix. These values are stored for population-level plotting and statistical testing.

The current implementation computes the following per-subject outputs:

  • Sample moments: sample_mu and sample_var of normalized energy (pooled across windows and regions).
  • Lognormality: multivariate lognormal fit log-likelihood (log_likelihood) computed on log(energy_norm + eps).
  • Equal Energy Distribution (EED): distribution similarity across regions using a combination of Jensen–Shannon divergence and KS distance (EED).
  • Coupling Ratio (CR): mean off-diagonal covariance relative to mean diagonal covariance of regional energy (coupling_ratio).
  • Energy Variance (VR): mean diagonal covariance of regional energy (energy_variance).
  • Brain Stability (BS): an aggregate stability score derived from the implied multivariate lognormal parameters (BS).

Quality controls

Energy metrics are sensitive to preprocessing failures and cohort inconsistencies. Recommended checks include:

  • Unit checks: verify comparable scale within modality before pooling.
  • Window parameter integrity: ensure consistent window length and overlap across subjects.
  • Label consistency: confirm channel or ROI order matches labels for every subject.
  • Outlier diagnostics: identify subjects with extreme distributions that may indicate artifacts.
Pooling is a modeling choice

Pooling across subjects increases statistical power but can hide subgroup structure. When appropriate, compute group PDFs and also inspect subject-level PDFs to ensure the population estimate is meaningful.

Outputs

Study-level energy metrics are written to the study PDO directory as a versioned MAT file that contains an outputest struct array. Each entry corresponds to one subject in the study PDI and includes the computed energy metrics along with identifying fields (patient code, diagnosis, modality, dataset). 

studies_store/<STUDY_NAME>/
  PDI/PDI_*.mat
  PDO/PDO_<SOURCE>_v<N>.mat    (contains: outputest)

Always treat the study PDI as the cohort snapshot that anchors each outputest artifact. If cohort membership or parameters change, recompute energy metrics to generate a new versioned PDO file.
