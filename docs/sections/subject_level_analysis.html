Subject-level analysis

Subject-level analysis visualizes and summarizes a single subject after preprocessing. In the BEI Toolbox, this view is driven by the latest versioned PDI file in the dataset PDI directory and uses the subject’s stored patient_data entry to compute a normalized energy time series and channel-wise empirical energy distributions.

Scientific objective

  • Construct a time-resolved representation of signal energy.
  • Estimate empirical energy distributions per channel or ROI.
  • Plot per-channel energy PDFs and a covariance inset.
  • Prepare standardized features for population inference.

Normalized energy time series

Given a preprocessed time series x(t,c) for channel or ROI c, windowed signal energy is computed as: 

E_w(c) = Σ_{t ∈ window w} x(t,c)^2

For comparability across channels within subject, normalization is often applied: 

Ê_w(c) = E_w(c) / max_w E_w(c)

The normalized samples Ê_w(c) across windows form an empirical distribution for each channel or ROI.

Important

Normalization emphasizes relative allocation patterns. If absolute scale carries physiological meaning, retain both normalized and raw energy representations.

Energy PDFs and coupling inset

  • Moments or robust summaries of energy distributions.
  • Channel or ROI variance across windows.
  • Distributional equality statistics across regions.
  • Entropy-based scalar summaries.
  • Coupling proxies derived from covariance or state matrices.

Quality control plots

  • Raw time series sanity checks.
  • Windowed energy trajectories.
  • Per-channel histograms or density curves.
  • Covariance matrix visualization when applicable.

Common failure modes

  • Unit inconsistencies between subjects.
  • Flat or truncated segments not removed during preprocessing.
  • Mismatched label ordering.
  • Window length too short or too long for modality dynamics.
Best practice

Always inspect subject-level plots before including a subject in study-level aggregation. Distributional artifacts at the subject level propagate directly into population metrics.

Output

Subject-level results may be stored alongside the PDO or cached separately for performance: 

datasets_store/<DATASET_NAME>/subject_features/<PATIENT_CODE>.mat
